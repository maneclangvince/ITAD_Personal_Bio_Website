Simple But Funny Childhood Experience

My childhood was simple and quiet(1). My mother and father lived with me in my grandmother’s house(2). Life felt ordinary during those early years(3). After some time, we moved into a new apartment(4). At first, we felt relief and comfort in our new home(5). However, we later realized that the apartment was haunted by crying ghosts(6). The strange experiences slowly became more disturbing as time passed(7). Some nights were filled with fear and confusion(8). After two years, we decided to move again to escape the hauntings(9). We believed the next apartment would finally be safe(10). Unfortunately, something more sinister appeared(11). It felt like a devil-like creature was present in the house(12). One day, we took a family picture together(13). The creature appeared in the photo as a dark shadow(14). The image looked corrupted, as if the phone itself was affected(15). We were terrified and could not stay any longer in that place(16). After one year, we moved into our current house(17). This time, the strange experiences finally stopped completely(18). I also remember playing outside with my childhood friends(19). We played games like patingtero and other traditional childhood games(20). Everything changed when the internet was introduced into my life(21). Online gaming became my main hobby and part of my lifestyle(22).

My Peak Teenage Years That I Wont Forget

My teenage years were shaped by my introverted personality(1). I found it very difficult to communicate with other people(2). It became even harder when someone did not match my vibe(3). Because of this, I often chose to stay quiet and keep to myself(4). Over time, I realized that I should at least try to communicate with others(5). I learned that no man is an island(6). Another important aspect of my teenage life was my attitude toward studying(7). During my high school years, I barely studied at all(8). I was the type of student who felt satisfied with a grade of 75(9). Unlike others, I did not think that a score below 90 meant failure(10). I often slept in class while teachers were discussing lessons(11). I also ate during class and sometimes left the room during recess(12). I did not fully cut classes because I was studying in a Catholic school at that time(13). Most of the time, I only did what was necessary to pass my subjects(14). Everything began to change when I entered senior high school(15). For the first time, I became serious and disciplined in my studies(16). I started putting in consistent effort and focus(17). From Grade 11 to Grade 12, I became a consistent honor student(18). My teachers noticed my improvement and felt proud of my progress(19). Their recognition motivated me to work even harder(20). Honestly, this transformation and achievement felt incredibly great and fulfilling(21).

My experience in college was pretty rough at the start(1). Everything felt overwhelming from the very first semester(2). I came from being a consistent honor student in senior high school(3). Suddenly, I was barely getting a 1.75 in my final period grades for my subjects(4). This drastic change affected my confidence a lot(5). One major reason was the new school system that I had to adjust to(6). The expectations and rules were very different from what I was used to(7). Every semester, I needed to adapt to new professors(8). Each professor had a different teaching style and grading system(9). It was also difficult to adjust to unfamiliar classmates and their behaviors(10). Because of this, I usually struggled at the beginning of every semester(11). However, I slowly improved as time went on(12). I learned that adjustment was a constant part of college life(13). I chose UE because it is well known for IT and Computer Science(14). Despite the difficulty, the experience taught me valuable lessons(17). One important lesson was to start projects early(18). This became crucial because of upcoming capstone projects(19). The program prepared me for better job opportunities in the future(20). It also helped shape a more disciplined IT lifestyle(21). I am truly thankful to my parents for giving me this opportunity and support(22).
